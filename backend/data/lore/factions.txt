MAJOR FACTIONS OF AETHERMOOR

THE THORNWOOD ROYAL COURT
Seat of power in the Thornwood Kingdom, based in Ashenveil City. Queen Seraphine Aldric rules with a council of advisors including Arcane Council Chair Eldrin Ashveil, Lord Commander Davan Holt of the Royal Guard, and Treasurer Lady Orynn Blackwood. The court is currently fractured over how to handle the border drought crisis — military hardliners want to seize Iron Reaches grain stores while moderates push for diplomacy.

THE ARCANE COUNCIL
The regulatory body for all magic use in Aethermoor, founded after the Sundering to prevent another catastrophe. Chaired by Eldrin Ashveil. The Council licenses mages, regulates magical artifacts, and maintains the Veil Observatories that monitor the Aetherial Current. Recently, several junior Council members have gone missing under mysterious circumstances.

THE IRONCLAD MERCENARY GUILD
Founded 460 AE by former Ironreach soldiers, the Ironclad Guild is the most powerful mercenary organization in Aethermoor. Based in the border city of Stonegate, they operate under strict contract law and have a reputation for absolute reliability once hired. Kira Stonefang commands their Ashenveil City chapter. The Guild maintains strict neutrality in political conflicts but has been quietly approached by both the Thornwood Court and Iron Reaches leadership as tensions rise.

THE SHADOW MARKET
An underground network of information brokers, smugglers, and fixers operating across Aethermoor's major cities. Mira Coldwell is a mid-level node in this network, though she'd never call it that. The Shadow Market's leadership is unknown but believed to operate out of the Shatterlands. They have been increasingly active as the drought crisis deepens, capitalizing on food shortages.

THE VEILBORN REMNANT
A secretive group claiming to be descendants of the mages who survived the Sundering. They have begun appearing in the Shatterlands and making contact with agents in Ashenveil City. Their intentions are unknown. The Arcane Council considers them extremely dangerous. Some believe they seek to restart the Aetherial Current ritual that caused the Sundering.
