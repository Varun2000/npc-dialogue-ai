WORLD HISTORY OF AETHERMOOR

THE AGE OF FOUNDING (1–400 AE)
The continent of Aethermoor was settled by three great peoples: the Thornwood humans of the western forests, the Ironreach clans of the northern mountains, and the Veilborn mages who emerged from the eastern desert. For four centuries, these three peoples maintained an uneasy peace through the Threefold Accord, a treaty that divided trade routes and magical resources between them.

THE SUNDERING OF THE VEIL (412 AE)
The Sundering was the most catastrophic event in Aethermoor's history. A group of Veilborn archmages — including the young wizard Eldrin Ashveil — attempted to permanently harness the Aetherial Current, the invisible river of magical energy flowing beneath the continent. The ritual failed catastrophically. The resulting explosion shattered the eastern continent, creating the Shatterlands — a fractured archipelago of floating islands still visible on the horizon. The magical backlash killed half the Veilborn population and destabilized magic across the continent for a generation. Eldrin survived but his mentor did not. He has lived with the guilt ever since.

THE IRON WARS (450–480 AE)
In the chaos following the Sundering, the Ironreach clans expanded aggressively southward, launching three decades of conflict known as the Iron Wars. The Thornwood Kingdom barely survived, saved only when the Ironclad Mercenary Guild — originally founded by Ironreach deserters — switched sides in exchange for trade rights. The Iron Wars ended with the Treaty of Stonegate, which established the current border between the Thornwood Kingdom and the Iron Reaches.

THE THORNWOOD KINGDOM TODAY (Current era: 891 AE)
The Thornwood Kingdom is the largest political entity on the western continent. Ruled by Queen Seraphine Aldric, it maintains a fragile peace with the Iron Reaches through the Ironclad Guild's neutrality. Magic has recovered from the Sundering but is closely regulated by the Arcane Council, which Eldrin nominally chairs. Tensions are rising due to a drought that has caused food shortages in the border regions and rumors of Veilborn survivors returning from the Shatterlands.
